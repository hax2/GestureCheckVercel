Create an 8-second gesture-learning video of one abstract meaning.

Match this reference style:
This instructional video uses a static, eye-level camera framing that captures the performer from head to toe. The subject is centered against a plain, neutral-toned studio backdrop. Soft, even lighting creates a clean instructional look. The performer uses clear, deliberate body movement at a steady pace, with the whole body available for the gesture when useful. The frame contains only the performer and the plain background. The instructional style is direct and minimalist, relying solely on the clarity of the performer's gesture to convey the lesson.

Gesture action:
The performer communicates intermittence with one clear full-body gesture. She traces a short horizontal path in front of her torso with one hand, then briefly marks three separated positions along that same path, with small pauses between each marked position. Her body remains available for the timing: a slight forward pulse may accompany each marked position. The whole movement should read as occasional points on a timeline, performed as one unified gesture.

Production constraints:
- One adult woman, whole body visible from head to feet at all times, centered in frame.
- Static camera, neutral indoor background, simple even lighting.
- Mouth closed at all times.
- Neutral face at all times.
- The meaning must be conveyed by the body and hands only, not by facial expression.
- She may use her whole body for the gesture, including leaning, stepping, shifting weight, bending, or changing posture when useful.
- The gesture should be clear enough for a second-language vocabulary lesson.
- Use natural human timing: start neutral, perform exactly one continuous gesture, then hold the final pose briefly.
- Keep the motion simple, repeatable, and pedagogically usable rather than theatrical.
- Avoid a chain of different gestures; do not mime a story or combine multiple symbolic actions.
